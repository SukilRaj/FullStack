Pluto

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet, is now classified as a dwarf planet located in the distant Kuiper Belt region of our solar system. It is a unique and geologically active world with a fascinating history and a complex system of five moons.

Why is Pluto no longer a planet? Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union because other objects might cross its orbit.
